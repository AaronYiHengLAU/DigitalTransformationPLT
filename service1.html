RAM part

DDR RAM Evolution (DDR-DDR5)

Understanding the evolution of DDR memory technology from DDR1 to the latest DDR5 for optimal system performance.

  • DDR1 (2000) - 184-pin, 200-400 MT/s transfer rates
  • DDR2 (2003) - 240-pin, 400-1066 MT/s with lower voltage
  • DDR3 (2007) - 240-pin, 800-2133 MT/s improved efficiency
  • DDR4 (2014) - 288-pin, 2133-3200+ MT/s mainstream standard
  • DDR5 (2020) - 288-pin, 4800-6400+ MT/s latest technology

DDR4 vs DDR5 Current Standards

Modern systems predominantly use DDR4 and DDR5 memory. Understanding the key differences between these generations.

  • DDR4: Mature technology, widespread compatibility
  • DDR5: Latest standard with doubled bandwidth capacity
  • DDR4: Lower cost, proven stability and reliability
  • DDR5: Higher performance, improved power efficiency
  • DDR4: Maximum 3200MHz standard, up to 5000MHz OC
  • DDR5: Starting at 4800MHz, up to 8000MHz+ OC

Data Rate & Performance Specs

Technical specifications comparing DDR4 and DDR5 performance metrics including MT/s, GB/s, and frequency ratings.

  • DDR4-3200: 3200 MT/s, 25.6 GB/s bandwidth
  • DDR5-4800: 4800 MT/s, 38.4 GB/s bandwidth
  • DDR4-3600: 3600 MT/s, 28.8 GB/s bandwidth
  • DDR5-5600: 5600 MT/s, 44.8 GB/s bandwidth
  • DDR5 offers 50% higher bandwidth than DDR4
  • Lower latency requirements for optimal performance
